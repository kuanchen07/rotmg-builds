Rogue mechanics — Lethal Strike & snapshot

Lethal Strike: Rogue is very unique compared to other builds: it focuses on a specific stat based on the cloak’s scaling, weapon rate of fire, and weapon total shots fired. With the exception of Planewalker, cloaks have a specific effect called Lethal Strike, and that’s mainly where rogue’s damage comes from. Lethal Strike fires 2 shots that deal damage equal to your lethal strike damage and shoot faster the faster your weapon shoots and the more shots it shoots. The Lethal Strike effect also applies additional flat damage to your weapon’s shots based on your lethal strike damage, and your shots deal extra % damage based on the target’s defense. The flat damage and the extra damage based on the target’s defense both scale with the cloak’s scaling stat (e.g. wisdom on that cloak). Additionally, Lethal Strike’s rate of fire increases relative to how fast you’re shooting, which makes enchants like Flurry of Blows (+35% rate of fire, −20% weapon damage) and dexterity valuable too.

Rogue’s main swapout is Planewalker because of its no-cooldown uniqueness compared to other cloaks, which allows for more uptime on Lethal Strike.

Snapshot: Another unique thing about rogue and its cloaks is that the moment you get the Lethal Strike effect, the game checks your stats (e.g. 150 wisdom) and uses them for the current cloak’s scaling — the same cloak you got Lethal Strike on. It also uses the Lethal Strike duration from that same cloak. This only lasts during the Lethal Strike duration, so if you swap to another item that gives less wisdom, it won’t affect the current Lethal Strike scaling, but it will affect the next one unless you swap back before getting the next Lethal Strike.
